Come & Listen To Our Informal Talks

Our programme will be lead by a number of established Great War historians who have become familiar both in print and on televison throughout the centenary period.

Friday 9th November Doors open 6:00pm for 6:30pm start

Get Tickets


The evening will consist of five abridged talks, with an interval and then a joint Q&A session to finish - think speed dating but with historians!


Joshua Levine: “On a Wing and a Prayer”

#GreatWar100 is excited to announce that Joshua Levine, author of On a Wing and a Prayer, and Forgotten Voices of the Somme, is going to join us this November. Aeroplanes were in their infancy in 1914, and he will give a talk on the pioneers of military aviation and their role in the Great War.


Phil Weir: “The royal Navy & the End of the War”

Two years after the Battle of Jutland, what was the Royal Navy up to and how did the conflict end for Britains sailors? #GreatWar100 are delighted that Phil Weir will be giving a talk on the Royal Navy at the end of the war, including the taking of the surrender of the German High Seas Fleet.


Peter Doyle: “Remembering Tommy”

So you have an ancestor that served in the war, but how much do you really know about what that experience entailed? #GreatWar100 is thrilled to announce that Peter Doyle, author of Remembering Tommy: The British Soldier in the First World War will join us to describe what life was like for Tommy from the time that he enlisted, until, if he was one of the lucky ones, his demobilisation at the end of the war.


Peter Hart: “The Last Battle: Endgame on the Western Front, 1918”
Names like Passchendaele and Ypres roll off of the tongue, but how much do you know about the end of the war? Peter Hart will describe the dramatic final weeks and just how Germany's surrender was pressed from a foregone conclusion into reality on 11th November 1918.


Alexandra Churchill: “Georgie, Willy and Nicky””

In 2018 Alex Churchill releases a unique account of the Great War experience with the first authorised biography of King George V in more than 30 years. In the Eye of the Storm: George V and the Great War explores the role of a constitutional monarch in war time and presents a wealth of new material. At #GreatWar100, we are glad to announce that Alex will be speaking about the King and his two cousins, and how the relationship between the three played out during the conflict.

Laura Burkinshaw: “Beyond Jutland”

#GreatWar100 is delighted to introduce Laura Burkinshaw. Although perhaps the most famous engagement of the Great War, naval warfare was far more diverse than the outcome of just one battle. This interesting history is often overlooked and Laura will be speaking about the Dover Patrol, early submarine warfare, and the cat and mouse game that developed from Admiralty attempts to combat this threat using decoy vessels, the top secret ‘Q Ships’ or ‘mystery ships.’

Dr. Vivien Newman: “Singers, Spies and Suffragists”

Not all women filled shells in the Great War. Prepare to be introduced to a formidable array of the fairer sex as they push against long established boundaries alongside Britain’s manhood in pursuit of victory.


Dan Hill: "The Big Picture: A Crash Course on the Western Front"

Most of us today know the names of iconic battles on the Western Front such as Loos, The Somme, Cambrai, and those at Ypres, but sometimes the links between each one and the bigger picture can be hard to see. Dan Hill will be leading an interactive talk on the war on the Western Front, placing each battle in context, explaining how warfare continually evovled and linking the entire four years of conflict into a single narrative. The front four rows won’t get wet - but will probably be conscripted. Not one to miss!


Alexandra Churchill: “King George V and The Great War”

In 2018 Alex Churchill releases a unique account of the Great War experience with the first authorised biography of King George V in more than 30 years. In the Eye of the Storm: George V and the Great War explores the role of a constitutional monarch in war time and presents a wealth of new material, including a new perspective on the Tsar's asylum claim, on the contribution of Edward, Prince of Wales, the proposed trial of the Kaiser in London and the death of Prince John. At #GreatWar100, we are glad to announce that Alex will be presenting a new talk about the King and his role in the war, and how it shaped the approach to monarchy of the Royal Family today.


Richard Van Emden: “1918: The Decisive Year in Soldiers Own Words and Photographs”

We are delighted that Richard will be joining us for #GreatWar100 to talk about his new book: 1918: The Decisive Year in Soldiers Own Words and Photographs. Revisiting the winning formula of diaries and memoirs, and above all original photographs taken on illegally-held cameras by the soldiers themselves, Richard tells the story of 1918, of both the ferocious spring offensive that so nearly brought victory for the Germans in the West, and the tenacious British rearguard fight that thwarted them. The book also tells the vivid story of the Allied breakthrough and the return to open warfare that was to bring victory in November 1918.


Guest Speaker Richard Van Emden


We are delighted that Richard will be joining us for #GreatWar100. He has interviewed nearly 300 veterans of the Great War and has produced a wealth of written material on the subject, including the phenomenally successful The Last Fighting Tommy. During the centenary period his titles have included: The Road to Passchendaele, Somme, Gallipoli, All Quiet on the Home Front and Tommy’s War. Richard has also contributed to over a dozen documentaries on the subject of The Great War, and has been an integral part of the BBC’s commemorative programming during the centenary.

Guest Speaker Alexandra Churchill


In 2018 Alex Churchill releases a unique account of the Great War experience with the first authorised biography of King George V in more than 30 years. 'In the Eye of the Storm: George V and the Great War' explores the role of a constitutional monarch in war time and presents a wealth of new material, including a new perspective on the Tsar's asylum claim, on the contribution of Edward, Prince of Wales, the proposed trial of the Kaiser in London and the death of Prince John.


Since 2014, Alex Churchill has published a number of books on the Great War, including volumes on the Battles on the Somme and at Passchendaele in collaboration with Andrew Holmes. She has appeared on a number of documentaries about the conflict, and formatted Channel 5's centenary effort for the sinking of the Lusitania in 2015.

Guest Speaker Peter Doyle


Peter Doyle has contributed to many documentaries about the war, and has published a number of books about diverse aspects of the Great War, including 'Fritz and Tommy: Across the Barbed Wire' and 'Kitcheners Mob: The New Army to the Somme' during the centenary period. In addition to being a Great War historian, Peter is an eminent geologist and as such has published specialised books about aspects of the war that include tunnelling and geology and trench warfare.
